* # AI Workflows / Triptych Redesign  
*   
* ## Product / Context  
* Enterprise AI workflow platform used to generate structured documents through chained prompts and step-based execution. The original system had been in production for over a year and was becoming a flagship capability shown to clients, but the interaction model remained difficult for non-technical enterprise users to understand. The product behaved more like a programmable prompt interface than a document creation tool.  
*   
* The redesign effort focused on modernizing the workflow experience and creating a more extensible interaction model that could support both non-technical business users and technical solution engineers.  
*   
* ---  
*   
* ## Your Role and Constraints  
* Led the design and definition of the triptych interaction model in close collaboration with product and engineering.  
*   
* Responsibilities included:  
* - Competitive analysis across AI and enterprise workflow products  
* - Research synthesis and interaction modeling  
* - User flows, diagrams, and prototype creation  
* - Leading critiques, testing sessions, and stakeholder alignment  
* - Iterating with engineering on feasibility and scalability  
*   
* Constraints included:  
* - Existing production architecture and legacy interaction patterns  
* - Need to preserve familiarity for current users  
* - Pre-MVP technical realities while simultaneously redefining product direction  
* - Diverse user audience ranging from non-technical enterprise users to technical IT solution engineers  
* - Designing around AI prompting behavior without requiring users to think like prompt engineers  
*   
* ---  
*   
* ## One Before / After Moment  
*   
* ### Before  
* Users could complete workflow steps, but they were not confident in what the system was actually producing or how the final document was being assembled. Prompting, editing, and generation all occurred in a single surface, making outcomes feel implicit and difficult to reason about.  
*   
* ### After  
* Users could see prompts being committed into visible document sections step-by-step. The triptych model separated structure, configuration, and document output into distinct layers, helping users understand how each action contributed to the resulting artifact and increasing confidence while progressing through workflows.

* # Original Workflow Model / Challenge Visual  
*   
* ## Product / Context  
* A legacy two-pane workflow interface where prompting, editing, and generation occurred within a single execution surface. Users progressed linearly through steps but lacked visibility into how outputs accumulated into a coherent document.  
*   
* This artifact exists in the case study to explain the foundational UX problem that motivated the redesign.  
*   
* ---  
*   
* ## Your Role and Constraints  
* Personally recreated and abstracted the original workflow model into a sanitized visual artifact for the portfolio case study.  
*   
* Defined the conceptual framing around:  
* - Collapsed execution surfaces  
* - Implicit system state  
* - Detached document assembly  
* - Prompt-heavy mental models  
*   
* Constraints included:  
* - Avoiding proprietary UI/IP exposure  
* - Preserving the “feel” of the original enterprise system  
* - Translating a dense workflow experience into a concise visual narrative  
* - Making the critique feel product-oriented rather than user-blaming  
*   
* ---  
*   
* ## One Before / After Moment  
*   
* ### Before  
* The original workflow allowed users to execute prompts and move through steps, but the interface gave little indication of how outputs connected together. Users could navigate the system without understanding the artifact being produced.  
*   
* ### After  
* The challenge visual reframed the issue as a systems problem instead of a usability complaint. It clearly illustrated that structure, execution, and document output were disconnected mental models concentrated into a single overloaded surface.

* # Resume Analysis Workflow Prototype  
*   
* ## Product / Context  
* Interactive prototype created to demonstrate how the triptych interaction model could support AI-assisted resume analysis and document generation workflows.  
*   
* The prototype simulates:  
* - Step progression  
* - Prompt execution  
* - Saved state behavior  
* - Real-time document assembly  
* - Completion flows and workflow confidence  
*   
* The use case was intentionally reframed around resume analysis because it is broadly understandable and easier to reason about than internal enterprise policy workflows.  
*   
* ---  
*   
* ## Your Role and Constraints  
* Designed and directed the prototype interaction model and narrative structure.  
*   
* Responsibilities included:  
* - Defining workflow states  
* - Designing interaction patterns  
* - Simulating AI execution behavior  
* - Mapping step progression into document assembly  
* - Designing completion and reinforcement patterns  
* - Directing front-end implementation through Cursor and iterative prompting  
*   
* Constraints included:  
* - Needed to simulate complex workflow behavior without a real backend  
* - Needed to communicate product thinking clearly inside a portfolio environment  
* - Had to balance realism with readability and narrative clarity  
* - Required careful abstraction away from proprietary enterprise content  
*   
* ---  
*   
* ## One Before / After Moment  
*   
* ### Before  
* Workflow execution felt abstract. Users interacted with prompts, but did not clearly understand how work accumulated into a finished artifact.  
*   
* ### After  
* Users could run prompts, save outputs, see sections committed to the document, and track progression visually across the workflow. The system reinforced confidence through persistent visibility of progress and document assembly.
